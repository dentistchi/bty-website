# Foundry MVP — 10 Structured Learning Programs

> Design-only. No code. Built entirely from the existing Arena scenario library.
> Every situation, key line, and practice fragment is sourced from real Arena scenario text.

**Philosophy:**
- Arena makes people act.
- Foundry makes them act better.
- Center helps them recover when they fail.
- Positive = action-generating. Negative = action-blocking. Growth = repeated action + delayed reward.

## Program 1: Skill Gap vs Mistake

### Core Theme
Most repeated errors in dental offices are skill gaps, not carelessness. Leaders who treat skill gaps like attitude problems create fear, not improvement.

### Why This Program Exists
Arena scenario `assistant_sterilization_errors_005` states the challenge directly: *"The leadership challenge is correcting behavior without creating a culture of fear. The decision point is immediate discipline versus retraining."* When players face this scenario, the most common first instinct is Choice A — issue a formal warning. But the Arena itself teaches: *"Discipline alone may not fix system or skill gaps"* (005-A microInsight). The same distortion repeats across billing errors (025), new hires (027), and procedure mistakes (022). Players keep reaching for accountability before checking whether the person was ever properly taught.

### Connected Arena Scenarios

| Scenario ID | Title | Source Role |
|-------------|-------|-------------|
| `assistant_sterilization_errors_005` | Assistant Repeatedly Makes Sterilization Errors | Primary situation |
| `new_associate_underperforms_015` | New Associate Doctor Underperforms | Primary situation |
| `new_employee_overwhelmed_027` | New Employee Feels Overwhelmed | Practice fragment |
| `assistant_mistake_during_procedure_022` | Assistant Makes a Mistake During Procedure | Practice fragment |
| `insurance_coordinator_billing_error_025` | Insurance Coordinator Makes Billing Error | Practice fragment |
| `staff_arrives_late_repeatedly_029` | Staff Arrives Late Repeatedly | Contrast case |

**Program Items:**
- S1-12 (EN: "Unsure about a sterilization step") — a staff member's uncertainty, not laziness
- L1_02 (KO: "Associate 생산성 저하: 코칭 vs PIP") — the leader's fork: coach or discipline
- L1_09 (KO: "신규 직원 온보딩 실패") — systemic onboarding failure

### Lesson Structure

**1) Situation**

Use the exact Arena framing from two scenarios:

Scenario 005: *"Sterilization logs show repeated mistakes. An instrument pack fails a compliance check. You feel patient safety anxiety. The hidden risk is regulatory consequences."*

Scenario 027: *"A new assistant struggles to keep up. The hidden risk: if you rush them, learning can be blocked. The integrity trigger is demand performance vs invest in growth."*

The connection: both look like performance failures. Both are actually gaps in how the person was taught. The sterilization assistant was never retrained after initial onboarding. The new assistant was never given structured guidance.

**2) Concept Video (2-min script outline)**

Open with the Arena's own question — scenario 005 forces you to choose between *"Issue a formal warning"* (A) and *"Schedule retraining and add a double-check step; no public blame"* (B).

Core teaching, drawn from Arena microInsights:
- *"Discipline alone may not fix system or skill gaps."* (005-A)
- *"Systems and training reduce errors more than blame."* (005-B)
- *"Seeing the process reveals system gaps."* (005-C)
- *"Pressure alone rarely speeds learning."* (027-A)
- *"People grow where they feel safe to learn."* (027-B)

The framework:
- **Mistake** = the person knows the right action, chose differently. Arena response: accountability + reminder.
- **Skill Gap** = the person never fully learned the right action. Arena response: *"Structured guidance: priorities, checklist, and regular check-ins"* (027-B label).

Close with 027's coachNote: *"Investing in growth with structured guidance builds capability and retention."*

**3) Scenario Practice**

- **Fragment A:** Play `assistant_sterilization_errors_005`. All four choices are live. The learner sees the Arena result for each path — especially the contrast between A (*"Fear may lead to hiding future errors"*) and B (*"Accountability is clear without shaming. Safety improves."*).

- **Fragment B:** Play `insurance_coordinator_billing_error_025`. The Arena context: *"An incorrect claim submission delays payment. The hidden risk: if you focus on blame, improvement may be blocked."* Key choice contrast: A (*"Blame rarely improves systems"*) vs B (*"Systems improve when ego steps back"*).

**4) Reflection**
1. *"What part did you contribute (even 1%) to the outcome?"* — from S1 `when_blame` reflection bank
2. *"What system allowed this to happen?"* — from S3 `when_blame` reflection bank

**5) Action Prompt**
Next time someone makes a repeated error, before correcting, use the Arena's own test: ask whether this is a discipline problem or a training problem. If the person was never properly taught, correction without retraining is wasted.

### Target Roles

| Role | Relevance |
|------|-----------|
| Doctor | High — scenario 005/022: directly frustrated by repeated assistant errors |
| Assistant | High — scenario 027: the one experiencing the gap |
| Admin / Front Desk | Medium — scenario 025: billing errors follow the same pattern |
| Manager | High — scenario L1_02: must decide coaching vs discipline |
| DSO | Medium — sets onboarding standards that create or prevent gaps |

### Trigger Logic
- Repeated low-XP choices on 005, 022, 025, 027 (choosing formal warning, criticism, or pressure over retraining)
- Repeated negative `communication` and `gratitude` hidden deltas on error scenarios
- S1 `when_blame` reflection trigger firing repeatedly
- L1 `when_blame` trigger: *"What expectation or system failed before the person did?"*

### Core XP Contribution
- **Communication mastery** — diagnosing before correcting
- **Leadership mechanics** — building training systems instead of blame systems

## Program 2: Listening Before Judgment

### Core Theme
The first response to disagreement is usually defense, not understanding. The quality of the next action depends on the pause between hearing and reacting.

### Why This Program Exists
Arena scenario `patient_questions_treatment_plan_021` captures this precisely: *"You may feel defensive, annoyed, or insecure. The hidden risk: if you react defensively, trust can be damaged. The integrity trigger is protect your ego vs protect the patient's trust."* When players face this, many choose A — *"Explain firmly why you are correct."* The Arena teaches: *"Being right can still cost trust"* (021-A microInsight). The same pattern repeats when a hygienist questions a diagnosis (003), when a patient complains about pain (028), and when a patient accuses overtreatment (009).

### Connected Arena Scenarios

| Scenario ID | Title | Source Role |
|-------------|-------|-------------|
| `patient_questions_treatment_plan_021` | Patient Questions Your Treatment Plan | Primary situation |
| `hygienist_questions_diagnosis_003` | Hygienist Questions Your Diagnosis | Practice fragment |
| `patient_complains_pain_after_028` | Patient Complains About Pain After Procedure | Practice fragment |
| `patient_overtreatment_accusation_009` | Patient Accuses Office of Overtreatment | Contrast case |
| `team_member_interrupts_024` | Team Member Interrupts You | Situation source |
| `assistant_feels_over_corrected_036` | Assistant Feels Over-Corrected | Perspective flip |

**Program Items:**
- S1-01 (KO: "환자가 30분 대기 후 불만 표출") — patient frustration, staff must listen first
- S1_13 (EN: "Patient complains about 10-min wait") — complaint under time pressure

### Lesson Structure

**1) Situation**

Use the exact Arena framing from scenario 021:

*"A patient says another dentist told them they do not need the crown you recommended. You may feel defensive, annoyed, or insecure. The hidden risk: if you react defensively, trust can be damaged."*

And from scenario 009: *"A patient questions the need for a crown. They mention another dentist disagreed. You feel defensiveness. The hidden risk is trust breakdown."*

Both scenarios present the same moment: someone challenges your professional judgment. The instinct is to explain, defend, or dismiss.

**2) Concept Video (2-min script outline)**

Open with the Arena's Choice A result from 021: *"You've stated your case but the patient may feel dismissed. Trust may not deepen."*

Core teaching, drawn from Arena microInsights:
- *"Being right can still cost trust."* (021-A)
- *"Understanding before defending protects trust."* (021-B)
- *"Dismissing the other opinion dismisses the patient."* (021-C)
- *"Transparency builds trust even when opinions differ."* (021-D)

The sequence the Arena rewards:
1. Receive without interpreting — 021-B label: *"Ask what concerns them most and listen before explaining."*
2. Reflect back — the follow-up prompt: *"Script: 'What's most concerning to you?'"*
3. Respond after understanding — 021-D: *"Offer to share X-rays or notes and suggest a second opinion."*

Close with 021's coachNote: *"Seeking understanding before defending your position protects the patient's trust and improves case acceptance."*

**3) Scenario Practice**

- **Fragment A:** Play `hygienist_questions_diagnosis_003`. Arena context: *"Maintaining unity in front of the patient while building clinical alignment."* The learner sees whether their choice listens or defends.

- **Fragment B:** Play `patient_complains_pain_after_028`. CoachNote: *"Listening fully before responding protects patient trust and guides appropriate care."* The key learning: pain complaints feel like accusations — but they're requests to be heard.

**4) Reflection**
1. *"What did you assume about their intent, and what else could be true?"* — from S2 baseline reflection bank
2. *"If you assume positive intent for 60 seconds, what changes?"* — from S1 `when_defensive` reflection bank

**5) Action Prompt**
In your next Arena scenario involving disagreement, before selecting, ask: *"Am I choosing the option that listens, or the one that defends?"* Then carry that question into your next real office interaction.

### Target Roles

| Role | Relevance |
|------|-----------|
| Doctor | High — scenarios 021, 003, 009: the one being questioned |
| Assistant | Medium — scenario 036: hears correction, needs to feel heard |
| Admin / Front Desk | High — first point of patient frustration (S1-01, S1_13) |
| Manager | High — scenario 024: mediates disagreements |
| DSO | Medium — receives upward feedback that is often dismissed |

### Trigger Logic
- Repeated selection of "explain firmly" or "defend/dismiss" in scenarios 003, 009, 021, 028
- Low `insight` hidden deltas across patient-facing scenarios
- S1/S2 `when_defensive` reflection trigger firing
- Pattern: highest-XP choice (listen first) consistently not selected

### Core XP Contribution
- **Communication mastery** — hearing before speaking
- **Perspective training** — understanding another person's frame before responding

## Program 3: Silent Team

### Core Theme
When people stop speaking up, the office doesn't get quieter — it gets more dangerous. Silence is accumulated frustration that surfaces as turnover, gossip, or burnout.

### Why This Program Exists
Arena scenario `team_member_feels_unappreciated_031` opens with: *"A hygienist says they feel invisible in the office."* The Arena's most common wrong choice is A — *"Explain that everyone feels busy and it's not personal"* — and the result is clear: *"You've defended the situation but they may feel unheard."* Scenario `assistant_feels_over_corrected_036` shows the next stage: *"Assistant says they feel micromanaged."* When both signals are ignored, the Arena shows what happens in 012: the assistant gossips about the doctor — silence turned toxic.

### Connected Arena Scenarios

| Scenario ID | Title | Source Role |
|-------------|-------|-------------|
| `team_member_feels_unappreciated_031` | Team Member Feels Unappreciated | Primary situation |
| `assistant_feels_over_corrected_036` | Assistant Feels Over-Corrected | Primary situation |
| `hygienist_burnout_010` | Hygienist Burnout Signs | Escalated version |
| `office_culture_divided_020` | Office Culture Divided | Context case |
| `assistant_gossips_about_doctor_012` | Assistant Gossips About Doctor | Consequence case |
| `you_realize_overreacted_045` | You Realize You Overreacted | Recovery path |

**Program Items:**
- S2_10 (KO: "동료 갈등") — conflict that went underground
- L1_11 (KO: "팀 내 피드백 방식 충돌") — feedback method breaking down
- S3_15 (KO: "팀 내 피드백 방식 충돌") — structural view of feedback failure

### Lesson Structure

**1) Situation**

Use the exact Arena framing, layered:

Stage 1 — Scenario 031: *"A hygienist says they feel invisible in the office. You may feel dismissive, surprised, or defensive. The hidden risk: if you dismiss this concern, morale can drop."*

Stage 2 — Scenario 036: *"Assistant says they feel micromanaged. You may feel defensive, reflective, or dismissive. The hidden risk: if you ignore their perception, engagement can drop."*

Stage 3 — Scenario 010: *"A hygienist seems disengaged. Patient complaints increase. You feel concern and tension. The hidden risk is turnover."*

The Foundry lesson connects these three Arena moments into a single arc: invisible → over-corrected → disengaged. Three stages of the same silence pattern.

**2) Concept Video (2-min script outline)**

Open with 031's key moment: someone says *"I feel invisible."* What do most people do? Arena Choice A: *"Explain that everyone feels busy and it's not personal."* Result: *"The concern is dismissed."*

Core teaching, drawn from Arena microInsights:
- *"Explaining away doesn't validate."* (031-A)
- *"Recognition builds stability."* (031-B)
- *"Explaining expectations doesn't address feeling micromanaged."* (036-A)
- *"Feedback works best when safe."* (036-B)
- *"Ignoring burnout rarely fixes it."* (010-D)

The silence sequence the Arena reveals:
1. **Signal**: someone says they feel invisible or over-corrected.
2. **Escalation**: they stop speaking up. Arena 010: *"disengaged. Patient complaints increase."*
3. **Consequence**: gossip (012) or resignation. Arena 010 coachNote: *"Addressing burnout with support builds retention and a culture where people can speak up."*

Close with the warning from 012's coachNote: *"Correcting gossip without humiliation protects culture and establishes respect standards."* Gossip is what silence becomes when people give up on being heard normally.

**3) Scenario Practice**

- **Fragment A:** Play `team_member_feels_unappreciated_031`. The learner faces: dismiss (A), acknowledge and ask (B), change subject (C), or give a generic thank-you (D). Arena teaches: *"Generic thanks without change can ring hollow"* (031-D).

- **Fragment B:** Play `assistant_feels_over_corrected_036`. Key contrast: reducing feedback (C: *"Less feedback isn't the same as safer feedback"*) vs asking what they need (B: *"Feedback works best when safe"*).

**4) Reflection**
1. *"What are you hoping will disappear on its own — and will it?"* — from S2 `when_avoidant` reflection bank
2. *"What delayed conversation can the team already feel?"* — from L1 `when_avoidant` reflection bank

**5) Action Prompt**
This week, ask the quietest person on your team one direct question: *"What's one thing we could do better?"* Then wait. Do not fill the silence. The Arena's own follow-up from 036-B: *"Ask what support they need and how you can give feedback better."*

### Target Roles

| Role | Relevance |
|------|-----------|
| Doctor | High — scenario 018/036: often the authority that silences without knowing |
| Assistant | High — scenario 031/036: most likely to go silent |
| Admin / Front Desk | Medium — first to notice behavioral changes |
| Manager | High — scenario 010: responsible for check-ins before burnout |
| DSO | Medium — turnover patterns trace to silent teams |

### Trigger Logic
- Repeated avoidant choices across 012, 020, 031, 036
- S2/L1 `when_avoidant` reflection trigger firing repeatedly
- Repeated low `gratitude` hidden deltas (not investing in people)
- Repeated "ignore it" or "explain it away" selections in relationship scenarios

### Core XP Contribution
- **Communication mastery** — creating conditions where others can speak
- **Leadership mechanics** — reading team signals before they become crises

## Program 4: Ownership vs Blame

### Core Theme
When something goes wrong, the first question is usually "Who did this?" The better question is "What allowed this?" System-thinking over person-blame is the foundation of a learning culture.

### Why This Program Exists
Arena scenario `front_desk_overbook_002` sets the stage: *"The schedule is double-booked for multiple high-production procedures. You feel stress, irritation, and unsupported. The hidden risk is publicly criticizing the front desk."* The most damaging choice is A: *"Call out the problem in front of the team and ask who did the booking."* Result: *"The front desk feels shamed. Morale drops and errors may go unreported next time."* The Arena's own microInsight: *"Public blame undermines psychological safety."*

The same pattern repeats in 025 (billing error: *"if you focus on blame, improvement may be blocked"*), 005 (sterilization: *"Fear may lead to hiding future errors"*), and 016 (HIPAA breach). Each time, the Arena teaches that blame stops learning.

### Connected Arena Scenarios

| Scenario ID | Title | Source Role |
|-------------|-------|-------------|
| `front_desk_overbook_002` | Front Desk Overbooks the Schedule | Primary situation |
| `insurance_coordinator_billing_error_025` | Insurance Coordinator Makes Billing Error | Primary situation |
| `assistant_sterilization_errors_005` | Assistant Repeatedly Makes Sterilization Errors | Practice fragment |
| `you_realize_overreacted_045` | You Realize You Overreacted | Recovery path |
| `hipaa_breach_incident_016` | HIPAA Breach Incident | High-stakes variant |
| `staff_arrives_late_repeatedly_029` | Staff Arrives Late Repeatedly | Contrast case |

**Program Items:**
- S3_08 (KO: "체어사이드 인수인계 누락") — handoff failure that looks like negligence
- S3 `when_blame` reflection bank — *"What system allowed this to happen?"*

### Lesson Structure

**1) Situation**

Use the exact Arena framing from scenario 002:

*"The schedule is double-booked for multiple high-production procedures. You realize the day is impossible to run smoothly. You feel stress, irritation, and unsupported. The hidden risk is publicly criticizing the front desk. The leadership challenge is addressing the operational error without shaming. The decision point is whether to react immediately or regroup privately."*

Pair with scenario 025: *"An incorrect claim submission delays payment. You may feel frustrated, blaming, or resentful. The hidden risk: if you focus on blame, improvement may be blocked. The integrity trigger is assign blame vs build process."*

Same moment, different domain. Both ask: do you find the person or fix the system?

**2) Concept Video (2-min script outline)**

Open with scenario 002's Choice A — *"Call out the problem in front of the team and ask who did the booking"* — and its result: *"The front desk feels shamed. Morale drops and errors may go unreported next time."*

Contrast with Choice B: *"Regroup privately with the front desk lead and fix the day together."* Result: *"You resolve the day without shaming anyone. The lead can own the fix and learn. Trust is preserved."*

Core teaching, drawn from Arena microInsights:
- *"Public blame undermines psychological safety."* (002-A)
- *"Private accountability builds systems, not fear."* (002-B)
- *"Avoiding the conversation avoids the fix."* (002-D)
- *"Blame rarely improves systems."* (025-A)
- *"Systems improve when ego steps back."* (025-B)

The follow-up from 002-B asks the real question: *"What protocol would prevent this next time?"* with options: *"Block rules and max procedures per day," "Daily schedule review the night before," "Single point of responsibility for booking."* This is what ownership sounds like — system fixes, not person fixes.

Close with 025's coachNote: *"Focusing on process rather than blame improves systems and retains talent."*

**3) Scenario Practice**

- **Fragment A:** Play `front_desk_overbook_002`. Four choices. The learner sees the full consequences: blame (A) destroys safety, private accountability (B) builds systems, shielding (C) buys time, avoidance (D) guarantees repetition.

- **Fragment B:** Play `you_realize_overreacted_045`. Arena context: *"After a tense interaction, you realize you were too harsh. The integrity trigger is protect ego vs apologize honestly."* This is the recovery path — what to do when you already chose blame. Arena's key line: *"Ownership builds maturity."* (045-B microInsight)

**4) Reflection**
1. *"What system allowed this to happen?"* — from S3 `when_blame` reflection bank
2. *"What would you do if you could not change them at all?"* — from S1 `when_blame` reflection bank

**5) Action Prompt**
After the next office error, use the Arena's own follow-up question from 002-B: *"What protocol would prevent this next time?"* Ask this before asking who was responsible.

### Target Roles

| Role | Relevance |
|------|-----------|
| Doctor | High — scenario 002: often assigns blame under clinical pressure |
| Assistant | Medium — scenario 005/022: on the receiving end of blame |
| Admin / Front Desk | High — scenario 002/025: most common blame target |
| Manager | High — responsible for building blame-free process systems |
| DSO | Medium — sets accountability frameworks |

### Trigger Logic
- Repeated selection of "call out publicly" or "criticize the person" in process scenarios (002, 005, 016, 025)
- S3 `when_blame` reflection trigger firing
- Repeated negative `communication` deltas on process-type scenarios
- Pattern of choosing person-focused over process-focused responses

### Core XP Contribution
- **Leadership mechanics** — systems that produce accountability without fear
- **Perspective training** — seeing process failures instead of person failures

## Program 5: Difficult Conversation

### Core Theme
The conversations that matter most are the ones people avoid the longest. A difficult conversation had early is a 5-minute talk. The same conversation had late is a crisis.

### Why This Program Exists
Arena scenario `doctor_chronic_late_007` makes it concrete: *"A doctor arrives late several times a week. Staff begin complaining. You feel the authority imbalance. The hidden risk is team resentment. The leadership challenge is addressing performance upward."* The most common wrong move is C: *"Avoid the conversation and try to absorb the impact yourself."* Arena result: *"Resentment builds. The doctor may not know the full impact. The pattern continues."* MicroInsight: *"Avoiding upward feedback erodes your credibility with the team."*

The same avoidance pattern appears in 017 (pay raise demand), 018 (anger in operatory), 033 (protocol resistance), and 041 (staff conflict). In every case, the Arena rewards the direct, private, respectful conversation — and penalizes delay.

### Connected Arena Scenarios

| `team_conflict_between_staff_041` | Team Conflict Between Staff Members | Situation source |
| `associate_disagrees_in_meeting_043` | Associate Disagrees in Meeting | Contrast (healthy version) |

**Program Items:**
- L1_04 (KO: "Front-Back 갈등 중재") — mediation requiring direct conversation
- L1_11 (KO: "팀 내 피드백 방식 충돌") — when feedback itself is the conflict

### Lesson Structure

**1) Situation**

Use the exact Arena framing from scenario 007:

*"A doctor arrives late several times a week. Staff begin complaining. You feel the authority imbalance. The hidden risk is team resentment. The leadership challenge is addressing performance upward. The decision point is whether to avoid the issue or schedule a candid talk."*

Layer with scenario 041: *"Two assistants argue over responsibilities. You may feel annoyed, concerned, or avoidant. The hidden risk: if ignored, culture can suffer. The integrity trigger is avoid conflict vs facilitate resolution."*

**2) Concept Video (2-min script outline)**

The Arena's own structure for the hard conversation (from 007-A follow-up):
1. State the observation → *"Schedule a private conversation and state the impact on the team and schedule."*
2. Follow up → options: *"Written expectation and consequences," "Check-in after two weeks," "Escalate to senior leadership or HR."*

Close with 041's coachNote: *"Mediating with clarity reduces conflict and strengthens culture."*

- **Fragment B:** Play `team_conflict_between_staff_041`. Key contrast: *"Tell them to figure it out themselves"* (A: *"Telling them to figure it out rarely does"*) vs *"Mediate calmly: hear both sides and help agree on roles"* (B: *"Leaders create clarity in tension"*).

**4) Reflection**
1. *"What delayed conversation can the team already feel?"* — from L1 `when_avoidant` reflection bank
2. *"What is a 2-minute version of the conversation you can start?"* — from S1 `when_avoidant` reflection bank

**5) Action Prompt**
Use the Arena's own follow-up from 007-A: identify the person, state the impact, and set a check-in. The Arena gives you the timeline: *"Check-in after two weeks."*

- L1 `when_avoidant` reflection trigger firing

## Program 6: Respect vs Authority

### Core Theme
Authority is a position. Respect is a behavior. When leaders rely on authority instead of earning respect, they get compliance — not commitment.

### Why This Program Exists
Arena scenario `doctor_anger_in_operatory_018` states: *"The doctor yells at an assistant during a procedure. A patient overhears the tension. The hidden risk is reputation damage. The leadership challenge is correcting a high-producer respectfully."* The temptation is A: *"Ignore it to protect production."* Result: *"The assistant and team may feel unsupported. Culture erodes."* MicroInsight: *"Ignoring protects revenue short-term, not culture."*

The same authority distortion appears in 043 (associate disagrees: *"if you shut them down, openness can suffer"*), 024 (interruption: the integrity trigger is composure vs dominance), and 004 (manager prioritizes production over clinical judgment).

### Connected Arena Scenarios

**Program Items:**
- L1 baseline reflection: *"What did your tone signal — safety or threat?"*
- L1 `when_control` Q1: *"Are you seeking compliance or commitment?"*
- S2 `when_control` Q3: *"What does 'firm + kind' sound like in your words?"*

### Lesson Structure

**1) Situation**

Use the exact Arena framing from two scenarios:

Scenario 018: *"The doctor yells at an assistant during a procedure. A patient overhears the tension. You feel cultural instability. The hidden risk is reputation damage. The leadership challenge is correcting a high-producer respectfully. The decision point is ignore behavior vs formal feedback."*

Scenario 043: *"Associate openly disagrees with your plan. You may feel threatened, defensive, or curious. The hidden risk: if you shut them down, openness can suffer. The integrity trigger is protect authority vs encourage dialogue."*

Both test the same reflex: when challenged, do you use your position or your behavior?
